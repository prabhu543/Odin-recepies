Palak Paneer

Palak Paneer is a popular Indian dish made by simmering paneer (cottage cheese) in a smooth palak (spinach) sauce. The combination of creamy paneer and the mild, slightly tangy spinach sauce creates a delightful and balanced flavor profile. This dish is often served as a vegetarian main course or as a side dish.

Ingredients:

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil, divided
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 6 cloves garlic, crushed
  • 2 teaspoons ground coriander
  • 2 teaspoons ground turmeric
  • 2 teaspoons garam masala
  • 2 teaspoons red pepper flakes
  • 2 teaspoons curry powder
  • 2 teaspoons ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 3 tomatoes, diced
  • 1 cup water
  • 2 tablespoons grated fresh ginger root
  • 2 cups cubed paneer
  • 2 (10 ounce) packages frozen chopped spinach, thawed and drained

Directions

  1. Heat 1 tablespoon olive oil in a skillet over medium heat; cook and stir onion in hot oil until slightly tender, about 5 minutes. Add garlic, coriander, turmeric, garam masala, red pepper flakes, curry powder, cumin, and salt; cook and stir until fragrant, about 1 minute.
  2. Mix spinach, tomatoes, water, and ginger into onion mixture; simmer for 20 minutes. Remove from heat and cool slightly, about 5 minutes.
  3. Transfer spinach mixture to a blender and blend until smooth. Set aside.
